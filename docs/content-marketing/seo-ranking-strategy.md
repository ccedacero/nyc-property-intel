- Server-render all content (no JS-gated text) so passage extraction works; add a 'Data sources & last updated' line per page ('Reflects NYC Open Data through [date]') for freshness signals.
- Put one short, extractable 'NYC Property Intel vs. PropertyShark / Marketproof' comparison table on the pillar (price + AI-query + honest data-vintage framing) - these queries surface directly in AI answers.

## Competitive Gaps to Exploit

- The competitive landscape shifted: the real competition is three new free-tier aggregator SEO sites (violationwatch.nyc, regwatch.nyc, dobguard.com - same playbook, possibly same operator) already ranking on 'free lookup by address, X agencies in one search' - NOT PropertyShark or gov sites. RegWatch claims '270+ government sources' and a '7-tab property profile', out-positioning the planned tagline on raw breadth.
- Therefore the moat is NOT 'many sources in one place' (now table stakes) - it is (a) natural-language AI query + MCP/agent access (literally no competitor can claim this) and (b) radical honesty about data vintage and limits. Lead every page with both.
- Investor/buyer angle on rent stabilization is wide open - ALL existing content (rentguidelinesboard, amirentstabilized, metcouncil) is tenant-facing ('is MY apartment stabilized'); nobody serves the buyer asking 'does this building I'm about to buy carry stabilization risk that reprices my pro forma'.
- ECB/violation buyer-side intent is under-served - all incumbents (expediters, title companies) cover removing/resolving violations, not finding judgment-lien exposure before the offer.
- Eviction-history and housing-court-litigation clusters are dominated by raw gov/open-data and academic sources with almost no commercial content - lowest-competition, highest-ease wins; litigation specifically is a capability (get_hpd_litigations) competitors don't surface.
- PropertyShark-alternative BOFU query has only MetroDeeds running the play well and we have zero presence - a dedicated page + free directory listings (G2/Capterra/SourceForge) is the fastest bottom-of-funnel win.
- AVOID as primary targets (all three analyses agree): Local Law 97 penalty/calculator queries (saturated with purpose-built calculators + outside our truthful capability set - building one would violate the honesty constraint) and the head 'who owns this LLC' term (JustFix Who Owns What is a beloved free incumbent; winnable only on investor long-tails). AI Overviews appear on essentially every informational query here, so GEO is mandatory not optional.
