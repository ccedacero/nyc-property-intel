# The Real Deal pitch

**Recipient:** tips@therealdeal.com
**Audience:** TRD covers NYC real estate news. Writers actively look for tech/tools angles.
**Goal:** A short writeup or "tools & toys"-style mention.
**Why this works:** TRD is the most-read NYC RE publication, and they actively cover proptech. The AI angle gives them a story.

## Email draft

**Subject:**
> NYC startup launches AI tool for real-estate due diligence — 22 city databases, one chat query

**Body:**

```
Hi TRD team,

Pitching a small NYC proptech story angle: I've launched NYC Property Intel
(https://nycpropertyintel.com), a free AI-powered tool that lets buyers,
investors, agents, and attorneys query 22+ NYC public databases for any
property in plain English.

The angle:

NYC has fantastic open data (DOB, HPD, ECB, ACRIS, DOF, 311, FDNY, NYPD,
marshal evictions, PLUTO, rent stabilization), but checking even 5–6 of
those for a single building takes 30–45 minutes of clicking through
disconnected city websites. NYC Property Intel uses Anthropic's Claude AI
to query all 22 databases at once via natural language.

Why this matters for TRD readers:

  - Levels the playing field for small landlords / first-time buyers who can't
    afford PropertyShark or Reonomy
  - Free + open-source under MIT license — anyone can self-host
  - Strict Fair Housing compliance built in at the software layer
  - Attorneys and pre-purchase due-diligence consultants are early adopters

Live demo (no install, 3 free queries):
https://nycpropertyintel.com/chat

Background:

  - Solo founder, NYC-based
  - Open source on GitHub: https://github.com/ccedacero/nyc-property-intel
  - Built on Anthropic's Model Context Protocol (MCP) — the open standard
    for connecting AI to external data sources
  - ~19M rows of NYC public data loaded into PostgreSQL, refreshed daily
    for primary datasets
  - Hosted version free for the trial period (10 queries/day for 30 days);
    paid pro tiers launching later this year

I think a "this NYC dev built X" angle would land well with your readers,
or a tools-roundup mention alongside other AI proptech tools.

Happy to send a 2-minute Loom walkthrough, demo a specific address you're
covering, or get on a 15-minute call. Whatever works.

Cristian Cedacero
cristian.cedacero@gmail.com
```

## Why this works

- TRD writers love NYC-specific angles. "Built by NYC dev" beats "another AI startup."
- Concrete proof points (number of databases, MIT license, MCP standard, rows of data) give them quotable stats.
- Mentioning specific competitors (PropertyShark, Reonomy) gives them context for the comparison they'll inevitably draw.
- Offer (Loom, address-specific demo, call) makes it easy to say yes.

## Follow-up

- Wait 7 business days
- Bump once with: "Hey — happy to provide additional info if helpful, or skip if not a fit. Either way, thanks."
- After 14 days: pitch Bisnow Proptech (different writers, same angle)
