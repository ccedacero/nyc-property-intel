# SPONY email pitch

**Recipient:** info@spony.org
**Audience:** SPONY = Small Property Owners of New York (~5,700 unit-owners). Active member newsletter + forum.
**Goal:** Get a member-newsletter mention OR a member walkthrough.
**Why high-fit:** SPONY members are EXACTLY who needs this — small NYC landlords who can't afford PropertyShark and need to check tenant complaints, DOB violations, and ECB on their own buildings.

## Email draft

**Subject:**
> Free due-diligence tool for SPONY members — check 22 NYC databases per address

**Body:**

```
Hi SPONY team,

My name is Cristian Cedacero — I built a free, open-source AI tool that lets
NYC property owners check 22+ NYC public databases for any address in one
query (DOB violations, HPD complaints, ECB violations, ACRIS, 311, sales,
permits, evictions, etc.).

I'm reaching out because I think it would be genuinely useful for SPONY's
membership of small property owners — many of whom (per SPONY's own surveys)
manage their buildings without the budget for paid tools like PropertyShark.

Live demo (no signup, 3 free queries):
https://nycpropertyintel.com/chat

Examples members would care about:
  "Any open HPD complaints on [my building]?"
  "Show me violations + 311 calls on [my address] last 12 months"
  "Has there been a DOB complaint filed I should know about?"
  "Comparable sales near my building"

Free trial after signup: 10 queries/day for 30 days. Self-host is free under
MIT license. Paid tiers for high-volume users will launch later this year.

A few details on alignment with SPONY's mission:

  - Strict Fair Housing compliance — the tool refuses tenant-screening
    queries at the software layer
  - Due-diligence research only — explicitly NOT an appraisal
  - All data from NYC Open Data with proper attribution
  - Open source on GitHub for full transparency

Two things I'd love to explore:

1. A 20-minute walkthrough I could record for SPONY's membership newsletter
   showing how a small landlord can use this for monthly building check-ups
   and pre-purchase due diligence.

2. Free unlimited access for SPONY members for the first 90 days post-launch,
   in exchange for product feedback that helps me build what small owners
   actually need.

I'd also be happy to come present at an in-person SPONY event if there's an
upcoming meetup where it would be useful.

Repo: https://github.com/ccedacero/nyc-property-intel
Site: https://nycpropertyintel.com

Cristian Cedacero
cristian.cedacero@gmail.com
```

## Why this works

- Specific to SPONY's mission ("small property owners") — they care about Fair Housing AND about tools their members can afford
- Concrete asks (newsletter, free access program) instead of "let's chat sometime"
- Demo URL up front so they can validate without scheduling
- Implicit acknowledgment that you understand their constraints (PropertyShark too expensive for small owners)

## Follow-up

- Wait 5 business days. If no reply, send a one-line follow-up: "Bumping in case this got buried — happy to chat when convenient."
- After 14 days, move on.

## Notes

- Don't BCC. SPONY values direct relationships.
- Use cristian.cedacero@gmail.com as the from address — using a @nycpropertyintel.com address before you've set up email forwarding will look fake.
- If they bite, ask for the format they prefer (sponsored newsletter slot? member-only post? video?)
